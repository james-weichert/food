My first attempt to make pad thai (almost two years ago) was a complete disaster—clumpy noodles, poor execution, a taste that completely missed the mark. After my first-try success with pad see ew, I decided to conquer my pad thai-making demons. The keys—once again—are to pre soak your noodles for a long time (it took me 1 hour), have a wok (it makes a difference!), and use the right ingredients, which includes tamarind paste and palm sugar. [This recipe](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=F86GfZIph8o) from _Hot Thai Kitchen_ was my north star, and I used a **1:1:1 ratio of tamarind paste, palm sugar, and fish sauce** to make the pad thai sauce. The taste is _almost_ there, but at least I finally have a solid foundation on which to improve. 

<strong class="tip">Tip!</strong> I had to make tamarind paste myself by soaking a block of [tamarind concentrate](https://sameday.hmart.com/store/hmart/products/21118177-tamarind-paste-14-oz) from H Mart following [these instructions](https://hot-thai-kitchen.com/making-tamarind-paste/).
