Named after the town of Amatrice, 65 miles away from Rome, Amatriciana is one of the four staples of Roman pasta (along with carbonara, cacio e pepe, and pasta alla gricia). Elevate a simple tomato sauce with traditional **_guanciale_ (cured pork jowl)** or bacon (in a pinch).
